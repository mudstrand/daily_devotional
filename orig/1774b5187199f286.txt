*Today’s Verse:*

*And since we are His children, we will share His treasures—for everything
God gives to *

*His Son, Christ, is ours, too. But if we are *

*to share His** glory, we must also share His suffering. *(Ro. 8:17)



*Today’s Reflection: *

There is a price to pay for being identified with Jesus. Along with the
great treasures, Paul mentions the sufferings that Christians must face. What
kind of sufferings are we to endure? For first century believers, there was
economic and social persecution, and some even faced death. We, too, must
pay a price

for following Jesus. In many parts of the world, Christians face pressures
just a sever

as those faced by Christ’s first followers.

Even in countries where Christianity is tolerated or encouraged, Christians
must

not become complacent. To live as Jesus

did—serving others, giving up one’s right, resisting pressures to conform
to the world—always exacts a price. *Nothing we suffer, however, can
comp**are—to
the great price that Jesus paid to save **us. *
